This build replaces the previous custom PCM5102A PIO with a backend adapted from
Raspberry Pi's pico-extras audio_i2s.pio, configured for standard Philips I2S.

What changed:
- Standard 16-bit stereo I2S timing
- Correct clock divider for BCLK = 32 * sample_rate
- Simple tone-test main.cpp (440 Hz square wave, always on)
- Initial non-zero priming frames to avoid zero-data mute during bring-up

Expected behavior:
- Audible continuous tone immediately after boot
- Onboard LED blinks at ~1 Hz

If still silent after this build, the remaining likely causes are hardware-mode straps
on the PCM5102A module (FMT/XSMT), power requirements of that breakout, or a pin-label
mismatch on the specific board.
