YUY0 optimization audit — pass B

Focus: DspChain hot-path tightening.

Changes applied:
- Chorus buffer changed to power-of-two (2048 samples) to replace modulo with bitmask wraps in the hot path.
- DspChain now caches last control values internally and ignores redundant setter pushes.
- DspChain maintains an active FX bitmask so process() does not re-query each module via float threshold checks every sample.
- Delay wet duck got a fast audio-rate setter used from AudioEngine, avoiding clamp/branch work on every sample because caller already guarantees [0,1].

Why these are coherent:
- They do not change architecture or timing model.
- They target recurring per-sample costs and cross-core/control-rate churn.
- They keep sound design behavior essentially intact while reducing hot-path overhead.

Known tradeoff:
- Chorus now uses 2048 samples instead of 1764, adding ~1.1 KB RAM, but enabling cheap wrap arithmetic.
